<h1>MAP for day 11 - MINIMAL</h1>
<h2>FOUR MINIMALIST PORTRAITS OF PORTUGAL</h2> 
<p>Inspired by the words of the 19th-century writer, traveller, and soldier James Edward Alexander - written after his visit to Portugal - I present four minimalistic ways to evoke my country, enclosed within its borders.</p>
<p>The first is the wonderful blue tilework of azulejo, from the Grand Lisbon Panorama before the Earthquake, housed in the National Tile Museum in Lisbon.</p>
<p>The second is the small egg custard tart, or pastel de nata, rightly found on any list of the world’s best desserts. (When J. E. Alexander was in Lisbon, he might already have tasted them, though he left no mention of it.)</p>
<p>The third is the <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rooster_of_Barcelos">Rooster of Barcelos</a>, a vividly painted clay figurine with an entertaining legend behind it.</p>
<p>The fourth is a staple of Portuguese cuisine: grilled sardines over burning coals, essential to the lively religious festivities held throughout the country every month of June.</p>
